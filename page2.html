My Personal Life

...the Philippines. I lived there simply and happily for about 6 years. Unfortunately, on 1991, a volcano called Mount Pinatubo erupted. Considered the second largest volcanic eruption of the 20th century, many homes -including mine- was destroyed. My sisters and I moved to the United States of America for safety and security. The event led to the most challenging times in my life. Though, experiencing such hardship taught me resilency and strength.

...is my family: My husband Cooper, 4-year-old son Bodin, and 2-year-old daughter Finley.

When I was 19-years-old, I took $200 and one carry-on luggage and moved to Seattle. Slowly but surely, I earned a Bachelor's degree in Biology from the University of Washington. Then, I earned my Master's degree in the Art of Teaching. I taught math and science for about 5 years. I feel fortunate to have had a career I enjoyed so much. Now, I am exploring a field I have been interested in for years: coding. I am relieved to find I enjoy it as much I thought I would. It's a lot to learn! But, I'm excited for this next chapter in my life.
